60 second mortgage market review

January 2009

60 second mortgage market review

Tags: 
Property

House prices finished the year up by 7% despite dire predictions of falls of up to 20% at the start of 2009. Confidence returned to some degree as the year progressed primarily as a result of the expected flood of repossessions failing to materialise and buyer demand outstripping the supply of property coming on to the market.

For 2010 however I believe growth is likely to be modest and would suggest a percentage rise of 2%. It is likely that the imbalance between supply and demand will continue into the early part of 2010, resulting in some further house price gains in the short term. However the combination of more properties for sale and modest, but increasing, interest rates will gradually exert a greater influence as the year progresses.

This downward influence is likely to be conditioned further by the still weak economy, high unemployment figures, and tight mortgage lending criteria. Some lenders have exited the market completely. Both funding conditions and lenders’ own appetite to risk have severely reduced the availability of mortgage funds. The end of the stamp duty holiday and the need for first time buyers to find a large deposit before getting on to the property ladder will further curtail any real improvement to market conditions.

The remortgage market is set to continue at subdued levels whilst low interest rates persist.
